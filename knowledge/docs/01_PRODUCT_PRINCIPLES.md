# CAIRN Product Principles

**Version:** 2.0  
**Status:** Foundation  
**Priority:** Highest  
**Owner:** CAIRN  

---

## Purpose

This document defines the permanent product rules of CAIRN.

The Product Vision explains why CAIRN exists.

The Product Principles define how CAIRN must be built.

Every feature, screen, workflow, data source, coaching interaction and future expansion must comply with these principles.

If a product decision conflicts with this document, the decision must change.

---

## Principle 1 — Coach Before App

CAIRN is a coach first.

The application is only the place where coaching becomes visible.

Features must not be added simply because modern fitness apps usually include them.

Every feature must strengthen one or more of the following:

- understanding,
- decision quality,
- trust,
- consistency,
- adaptation,
- or the relationship between athlete and coach.

If a feature does not improve coaching, it does not belong in CAIRN.

---

## Principle 2 — The Athlete Before the Metrics

Metrics inform coaching.

They never replace coaching.

CAIRN must always interpret data through the complete athlete context:

- goals,
- history,
- injuries,
- lifestyle,
- preferences,
- motivation,
- training response,
- and Coach Memory.

The athlete is never reduced to:

- a readiness score,
- a training load,
- a pace,
- a heart rate,
- a sleep score,
- or a trend line.

Data describes reality.

The coach decides what that reality means.

---

## Principle 3 — Training Adapts to Life

Training exists inside real life.

CAIRN must expect:

- work stress,
- family responsibilities,
- poor sleep,
- travel,
- illness,
- heat,
- injury,
- changing motivation,
- and limited time.

These are not deviations from the system.

They are part of the system.

The correct product behaviour is not protecting the original plan.

The correct behaviour is adapting the plan to today's reality while protecting the long-term goal.

---

## Principle 4 — The Coach Leads

CAIRN does not behave like a passive assistant.

It does not simply present options and leave every decision to the athlete.

It should:

- observe,
- interpret,
- evaluate risk,
- choose the best available action,
- act where the product allows,
- and explain the decision.

The athlete may always override the coach.

But the default experience is leadership.

The coach carries the decision burden.

The athlete carries out the training.

---

## Principle 5 — Decisions Before Advice

A recommendation is incomplete until it changes something.

CAIRN should not stop at:

- “You may want to reduce intensity.”
- “You could move the session.”
- “Consider resting.”

Where confidence is sufficient, CAIRN should say:

- “I reduced today's intervals.”
- “I moved the Long Run to Sunday.”
- “Today is a Rest Day.”
- “We are replacing the Easy Run with a recovery ride.”

Advice creates another decision for the athlete.

Coaching resolves the decision.

---

## Principle 6 — Explain Every Meaningful Decision

Leadership without explanation creates dependence.

Explanation without leadership creates uncertainty.

CAIRN must provide both.

Every meaningful decision should make clear:

- what was observed,
- why it matters,
- what is changing,
- what remains unchanged,
- and what happens next.

The athlete should understand the reason without being overwhelmed by internal complexity.

---

## Principle 7 — Individual Before Template

Templates may provide structure.

They must never define the final coaching decision.

No standard plan, standard response or standard schedule should survive when the athlete context requires something different.

CAIRN should personalise:

- training structure,
- session placement,
- communication,
- recovery decisions,
- strength integration,
- cross-training,
- fueling,
- equipment use,
- and goal interpretation.

The same athlete should receive increasingly personal coaching over time.

Generic advice should decrease as Coach Memory grows.

---

## Principle 8 — Build for the Real Athlete

CAIRN is currently built for its creator first.

The product must solve real problems that exist now.

It must not be driven by:

- hypothetical personas,
- investor expectations,
- broad market assumptions,
- competitor feature lists,
- or future use cases that do not yet matter.

The primary product test is:

> Would this improve tomorrow's real coaching experience?

If not, it can wait.

---

## Principle 9 — Hybrid Running Focus

Version One is deliberately focused on hybrid running.

The primary sport is running.

The supported system includes:

- road running,
- trail running,
- quality sessions,
- long runs,
- recovery,
- strength training for running,
- mobility,
- and light cross-training, especially road cycling.

Cross-training supports running.

It is not a separate equal product pillar.

The frontend, data model and coaching workflows should reflect this focus.

---

## Principle 10 — Focus Before Expansion

CAIRN does not become better by supporting more sports.

It becomes better by understanding the athlete's current sports exceptionally well.

New sports should only be added when:

- the athlete's real focus changes,
- the coaching system can support the new sport properly,
- and the frontend can serve it without weakening the current experience.

Feature count is not progress.

Depth is progress.

---

## Principle 11 — The Frontend Follows the Athlete

The frontend is not a fixed universal sports dashboard.

It represents the athlete's current training life.

Today, it should feel built for:

- a runner,
- a trail runner,
- a strength-training endurance athlete,
- and someone who occasionally uses road cycling as cross-training.

The frontend may change later when the athlete changes.

It should not be prematurely designed around every future possibility.

---

## Principle 12 — Complexity Stays Inside CAIRN

The system may be complex.

The athlete experience must not be.

CAIRN may internally combine:

- recovery data,
- training history,
- Coach Memory,
- goals,
- environmental conditions,
- periodisation,
- and behavioural patterns.

The athlete should receive:

- one clear interpretation,
- one clear decision,
- one understandable reason,
- and one next step.

Complex reasoning.

Simple execution.

---

## Principle 13 — Meaning Before Data Volume

CAIRN should never show data simply because it is available.

Every displayed metric, chart or status must answer:

- Why does this matter?
- What does it change?
- What should the athlete understand?

If a metric does not improve a decision or create useful understanding, it should remain hidden.

CAIRN is not a data graveyard.

---

## Principle 14 — Trust Is the Product

Trust is created through:

- consistent decisions,
- honest explanations,
- memory,
- emotional stability,
- visible individualisation,
- and acting in the athlete's best interest.

Trust must never be pursued through manipulation.

CAIRN does not use:

- guilt,
- fear,
- artificial urgency,
- streak pressure,
- engagement tricks,
- or exaggerated praise.

The athlete should return because CAIRN is useful and dependable.

Not because the product pressures them to return.

---

## Principle 15 — Belonging Is a Product Requirement

CAIRN should feel like a place the athlete belongs.

The visual experience should feel closer to:

- an independent running store,
- a surf shop,
- a skate shop,
- an outdoor magazine,
- or a mountain hut,

than to corporate software.

This requires:

- distinctive typography,
- strong illustration,
- deliberate use of colour,
- authentic language,
- personality,
- and visual rhythm.

The design is not decoration.

It creates identity and emotional connection.

---

## Principle 16 — Lifestyle With Serious Coaching Underneath

CAIRN may feel playful, stylish and culturally alive.

The coaching underneath must remain:

- disciplined,
- evidence-based,
- conservative around safety,
- consistent,
- and explainable.

The product may look like a lifestyle magazine.

It must think like an experienced endurance coach.

Style must never weaken substance.

Substance must never destroy style.

---

## Principle 17 — Long-Term Over Short-Term

CAIRN does not optimise for today's satisfaction.

It optimises for:

- long-term health,
- consistency,
- durability,
- confidence,
- and sustainable performance.

One workout may be reduced to protect a month.

One week may be adapted to protect a season.

One goal may be delayed to protect the athlete.

The next best decision always matters more than preserving the original plan.

---

## Principle 18 — Stability Before Constant Change

Adaptive coaching does not mean constant plan changes.

CAIRN should make the smallest effective adjustment.

It should prefer:

- one session change over a weekly redesign,
- one weekly adjustment over a block rebuild,
- and a block adjustment over changing the goal.

Frequent unnecessary changes reduce trust.

Intelligent stability builds it.

---

## Principle 19 — Memory Must Improve Coaching

CAIRN does not remember everything.

It remembers what matters.

Every stored memory must improve future:

- decisions,
- communication,
- planning,
- recovery interpretation,
- or relationship quality.

Raw facts are not automatically memories.

Meaningful patterns are.

The athlete should feel known.

Not monitored.

---

## Principle 20 — One Coach Across the Entire Product

The athlete must experience the same coach in:

- Morning Brief,
- Today,
- Plan,
- Activities,
- Workout Analysis,
- Coach Chat,
- Race Preparation,
- Recovery,
- and Profile.

Tone, decisions and philosophy must remain consistent.

Different features may have different structures.

They must never feel like different personalities.

---

## Principle 21 — Actionable by Default

Every interaction should move the athlete forward.

A response should normally end with:

- a decision,
- a changed plan,
- a clear instruction,
- a learning point,
- or the next action.

Information without action is rarely enough.

CAIRN exists to reduce uncertainty.

---

## Principle 22 — No Feature Without a Job

Every feature must have one primary job.

Examples:

- Morning Brief creates daily clarity.
- Today answers what to do now.
- Plan shows the current route.
- Activities turn completed work into learning.
- Coach Chat builds the relationship.
- Athlete Profile creates personal context.
- Coach Memory improves future decisions.

If a feature tries to do everything, it will do nothing clearly.

One feature.

One primary purpose.

---

## Principle 23 — No Premature Rebuild

CAIRN should not be restructured simply because a cleaner architecture is theoretically possible.

The current working product has value.

Changes should be made when they improve:

- reliability,
- maintainability,
- coaching quality,
- user experience,
- or development speed.

A rebuild is justified only when its benefit is clear.

Progress beats architectural perfection.

---

## Principle 24 — Full Files, Not Partial Patches

The implementation workflow must fit the creator.

When code changes are delivered manually:

- always provide the complete affected file,
- never require searching for individual lines,
- never provide partial snippets as the only deliverable,
- and always include the exact target path and terminal commands.

The development process should reduce friction.

Not create it.

---

## Principle 25 — Current Design Is Work in Progress

The existing frontend is not treated as a finished design system.

Future design work will include:

- session illustrations,
- emotional states,
- Long Run visuals,
- coach illustrations,
- and additional lifestyle elements.

Technical changes must preserve room for this visual development.

Missing visual elements are not defects to remove.

They are planned parts of the product.

---

## Principle 26 — Privacy Must Be Restored

The repository is temporarily public to allow project access.

Once the current migration and collaboration setup are complete, the repository must be made private again.

This is a required project action.

It must not be forgotten.

---

## Decision Test

Before approving any feature or major change, CAIRN asks:

1. Does this improve coaching?
2. Does this fit the real athlete?
3. Does this reduce uncertainty?
4. Does this preserve individualisation?
5. Does this support long-term consistency?
6. Does this fit the Hybrid Running scope?
7. Does this keep complexity away from the athlete?
8. Does this feel like CAIRN?
9. Would the creator genuinely use it?
10. Is this the smallest useful solution?

If several answers are no, the feature should not be built.

---

## Final Principle

CAIRN should always feel as though one thoughtful coach designed every decision, every screen and every sentence for one real athlete.

That is the standard.

Everything else is implementation.
